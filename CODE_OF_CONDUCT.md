# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers of Odyssey Learns pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

* **Being Respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
* **Being Collaborative**: Working together constructively
* **Being Empathetic**: Showing empathy toward other community members
* **Being Constructive**: Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* **Being Accountable**: Accepting responsibility for our mistakes and learning from them
* **Being Focused**: Prioritizing what is best for the community and the children we serve
* **Being Professional**: Maintaining professionalism in all interactions

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
* Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as physical or electronic addresses, without explicit permission
* Conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or educational setting
* Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior

## Child Safety

As a platform serving children, we have additional responsibilities:

* **COPPA Compliance**: All features must comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act
* **Safety First**: Any concerns about child safety must be reported immediately
* **Content Standards**: All content must be age-appropriate and educational
* **Parent Privacy**: Respect parent authority and privacy regarding their children
* **No Direct Child Contact**: Contributors should not attempt to directly contact child users

If you become aware of any potential safety issues, please report them immediately to security@odysseylearns.com

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing a project or community include:

* Using an official project e-mail address
* Posting via an official social media account
* Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event
* Contributions to the codebase, documentation, or discussions
* Participation in Discord, GitHub Discussions, or other community forums

Representation of a project may be further defined and clarified by project maintainers.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team at conduct@odysseylearns.com. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary and appropriate to the circumstances.

When reporting, please include:

* Your contact information
* Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved
* Your account of what occurred
* Any additional context or supporting documentation
* Whether you believe the situation is ongoing

The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident. Further details of specific enforcement policies may be posted separately.

### Response

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident. Reporters should receive an acknowledgment of their report within 48 hours.

### Investigation

The project team will:

1. Review the report and any supporting evidence
2. Consult with relevant parties while protecting reporter anonymity
3. Determine if the Code of Conduct was violated
4. Decide on appropriate action
5. Communicate the decision to relevant parties

### Consequences

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

Community members who violate this Code of Conduct may face consequences including but not limited to:

**Warning**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.

**Temporary Ban**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.

**Permanent Ban**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.
